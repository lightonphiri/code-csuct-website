Home     Research     Projects        


Rapid technological advances and more specifically Web technologies have given rise to different Digital Library System architectural designs. The varying designs can largely be attributed to the different design goals and problems the solutions were aimed at addressing. However, most of the existing systems are arguably complex, effectively making them difficult to extend, reuse and maintain.
The core of this research is focused on deriving domain specific design principles which may result in the implementation of simple architectures. It is premised that designs based on a simple and minimalistic approach may more easily be adopted and managed over time.
